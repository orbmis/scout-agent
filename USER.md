## Active hypotheses

- Verification gating and settlement determination are independently designable layers, and most current agent payment proposals conflate them
- The agent wallet stack will consolidate around ERC-4337 plus ERC-7702 (and soon EIP-8141) plus a delegation standard from the 7710/7715 family
- MEV-style centralisation pressures will reappear at the agent intent and solver layer, with similar supply-chain dynamics to current Ethereum block building
- Agent identity and attestation will be a critically important primitive in agentic commerce, not an afterthought layered on later
- Card rails will lose share to native crypto rails as agents take over, not because crypto is cheaper but because per-decision authorisation favours programmable rails
- Account abstraction adoption is gated by wallet UX and developer ergonomics rather than by standards completeness
- Reputation protocols for agentic commerce are interesting, but not valueable as cryptograhpically binding asset encumbrances or policy enforcement mechanisms (rationale: agents are cattle, not pets)
